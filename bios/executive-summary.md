### Who is Tyrone Nelms?

An operations and AI systems consultant and the founder of AJ Digital. His career is a stacked progression rather than a single lane: enterprise customer operations built the foundation (documentation, escalation handling, CRM discipline); operations and field roles added real-world execution; creative and digital marketing added brand and acquisition; AI-enabled systems work is the current synthesis.

### What problems does he solve?

Service businesses running on scattered tools, undocumented processes, and reactive workflows. Typical symptoms: follow-up that depends on memory, CRM records nobody trusts, processes that live in one person's head, automation experiments that stalled, and customer experience that varies by who answers.

### Who should hire him — and why?

- **Founder-led and service-based businesses** that need their operations documented, systematized, and selectively automated — by someone who has personally worked support floors, warehouses, field routes, and campaigns.
- **Organizations filling senior operations, project management, or customer-experience roles** that want enterprise support discipline (verified record: top-performer recognition, SME and team-lead roles, 99%+ adherence at Alorica) combined with modern systems and AI fluency.

### What differentiates him?

1. **Operator-turned-consultant.** The advice comes from having done the jobs the systems are for.
2. **Systems over tools.** Documentation and workflow design first; tool selection second.
3. **Evidence discipline.** No claimed outcome without support — unverified numbers stay out of his own materials, and client systems are built to the same standard.
4. **End-to-end service-business range.** Marketing, creative production, field operations, and back office — the whole surface a small business runs on.

### Credentials

Google professional certificates in Project Management, UX Design, and Digital Marketing & E-commerce (Coursera-verified). 15+ years across the roles in the canonical career history.
