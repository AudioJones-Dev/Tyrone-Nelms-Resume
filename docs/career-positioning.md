# Career Positioning

Strategic layer above the canonical records. Every outward-facing artifact — resumes, LinkedIn, website, proposals, speaker bios, capability statements — should derive its framing from this file, and its facts from [`career-history.md`](career-history.md) and [`accomplishments.md`](accomplishments.md).

This file contains positioning strategy, not new facts. Nothing here overrides the canonical chronology, and nothing here may introduce claims that do not trace to a canonical record.

## Core Positioning Statement

Tyrone Nelms is an operations and AI consultant who helps founder-led and service-based businesses turn scattered tools and reactive workflows into clear operating systems — combining 15+ years of enterprise customer operations, field service, digital marketing, and creative production with current AI-enabled systems work through AJ Digital.

## The Narrative Arc

From the canonical career narrative: the career is a stacked progression, not a single-lane chronology.

1. **Foundation — enterprise customer operations** (TigerDirect, Alorica, UnitedHealthcare): documentation, escalation handling, CRM usage, service quality, process discipline.
2. **Execution — operations and field roles** (AHLO, Florida Ramp & Lift): logistics, safety awareness, real-world service delivery, administrative systems.
3. **Reach — creative and digital marketing** (Miami Spin 360, AJ Digital marketing work): brand strategy, media production, content systems, customer acquisition.
4. **Synthesis — AI-enabled business systems** (AJ Digital consulting): using technology, systems thinking, and operational knowledge to improve business performance.

The positioning claim is that layers 1–3 are what make layer 4 credible: the AI consulting is grounded in having personally worked the phones, the warehouse, the field, and the campaigns that AI systems are meant to improve.

## Positioning Pillars

1. **Operator-turned-consultant.** Advice comes from lived operational experience, not theory. Evidence: the full chronology in `career-history.md`.
2. **Systems over tools.** Emphasis on workflow design, documentation, and process architecture rather than tool evangelism. Evidence: AJ Digital responsibilities in `experience/aj-digital.md`.
3. **Customer-experience literacy.** Enterprise-grade support discipline (first-call resolution, QA, escalation handling, 99%+ adherence at Alorica). Evidence: `experience/alorica.md`, `experience/unitedhealthcare.md`.
4. **Full-stack service-business range.** Marketing, creative production, field operations, and back office — the whole surface a small service business actually runs on.
5. **Evidence discipline.** Unverified metrics stay out of public claims (see Resolved Decisions in `career-history.md`). This is itself a differentiator: credibility as a brand value.

## Language Register

- Default register: accessible business language ("workflow automation", "business systems", "customer experience systems").
- Technical register ("AI operations", "agents", "orchestration") only for AI-specific audiences — same rule as the LinkedIn guardrails in `linkedin/profile-draft.md`.
- Never use internal codenames (e.g., Founder Intelligence Systems) with audiences that have no context for them, per the guardrails in `target-roles.md`.

## Positioning Guardrails (inherited, restated)

- Do not claim enterprise AI leadership without specific evidence.
- Do not imply formal software engineering employment.
- Do not overstate field technician certification scope.
- Do not use confidential client details without explicit approval.
- Do not present unverified metrics (tracked in `accomplishments.md` → Metrics to Verify) as fact anywhere public.
