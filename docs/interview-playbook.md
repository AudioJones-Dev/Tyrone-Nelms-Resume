# Interview Playbook

Preparation layer for interviews, discovery calls, and client conversations. Framing derives from [`career-positioning.md`](career-positioning.md); facts derive from [`career-history.md`](career-history.md) and [`accomplishments.md`](accomplishments.md).

Rule: every story told in an interview must trace to a canonical record. Where a result is qualitative or a metric is unverified, tell the story qualitatively — do not improvise numbers.

## Core Stories (STAR skeletons)

Each story is a skeleton to rehearse, not a script. All are built only from verified accomplishments.

### 1. Escalation ownership — Alorica

- **Situation:** Senior commercial-account role handling complex caller issues (contracts, fire code occupancies, multi-step resolutions).
- **Task:** Drive first-call resolution and handle supervisor callbacks, escalations, and special cases.
- **Action:** Used Salesforce, RightNow, SharePoint, and internal systems (STMS, OMS, RIO, DocDNA); documented issues end-to-end; made outbound calls to clients, dealers, and technicians.
- **Result (verified):** Employee of the Month (March 2015), quarterly top performer, TPSM Team SME, team lead, two years perfect attendance, 99%+ monthly adherence.
- **Use for:** reliability, ownership, process discipline, CRM fluency questions.

### 2. Mentoring and team support — Alorica

- **Situation:** High-volume support environment with continuous new-hire onboarding.
- **Task:** Support team performance beyond individual metrics.
- **Action:** Trained, mentored, and coached team members; provided new-hire floor support; hosted team huddles to communicate updates.
- **Result (verified):** SME and team-lead recognition alongside individual performance awards.
- **Use for:** leadership-without-title, coaching, and team-culture questions.

### 3. Research-to-resolution — UnitedHealthcare

- **Situation:** Healthcare provider support with complex payment and claims questions.
- **Task:** Resolve provider issues accurately in a regulated environment.
- **Action:** Researched customer and provider issues to identify root problems; applied quality assurance practices; handled difficult calls professionally.
- **Result (qualitative):** Positive customer experiences through QA discipline — tell qualitatively; no verified metric exists.
- **Use for:** problem-solving under constraint, difficult-conversation, and healthcare-adjacent questions.

### 4. Wearing every hat — AHLO

- **Situation:** Long-running relationship with a small business across warehouse, office administration, sales, and operations functions.
- **Task:** Keep operations, sales, and customer service running across roles and periods.
- **Action:** Order management, shipping/receiving, customer communication across channels, error correction and notation for other workers, daily operations direction.
- **Result (qualitative):** Trusted with progressively broader functions over multiple engagements. The 15% productivity metric is unverified — do not cite it; describe the systems work instead.
- **Use for:** small-business range, adaptability, and "tell me about wearing multiple hats" questions.

### 5. Founder-side systems work — AJ Digital

- **Situation:** Founder-led service businesses with scattered tools and reactive workflows.
- **Task:** Strategic consultation and operational management for clients.
- **Action:** Discovery, workflow documentation, CRM/automation strategy, project management in ClickUp/Asana/Notion, content and ad management, AI-supported workflow design.
- **Result (qualitative):** Streamlined workflows and improved team efficiency — qualitative until client-approved outcomes exist (tracked in `case-studies.md`).
- **Use for:** consulting approach, AI-practicality, and client-discovery questions.

### 6. Field reality — Florida Ramp & Lift

- **Situation:** ADA accessibility and mobile-lift service operations as an independent contractor.
- **Task:** Technical support, maintenance, logistics coordination, customer communication.
- **Action:** Field execution with safety standards; contributed to marketing and operational process improvements.
- **Result (qualitative):** The 20% cost-reduction metric is unverified — do not cite it. Emphasize hands-on service-business operations instead.
- **Use for:** practicality, safety-awareness, and "have you actually done operations" questions.

## Handling Known Soft Spots

Honest, prepared answers for the things the record genuinely leaves fuzzy:

- **"Your dates for Alorica/UnitedHealthcare are approximate?"** — Yes; these roles ended years ago and I list them year-only rather than guess precision. Exact records can be verified through employment verification if needed. (Matches the Resolved Decisions in `career-history.md`.)
- **"You had overlapping roles?"** — Correct: AJ Digital, Miami Spin 360, and Florida Ramp & Lift are concurrent by design — a consulting practice, a creative-production engagement, and a field-service contract. That mix is the point of the positioning, not an inconsistency.
- **"Why no degree?"** — High school diploma (American Academy, 2005) plus three Google professional certificates (UX Design, Project Management, Digital Marketing & E-commerce) with verification links, plus 15+ years of record. Lead with the record.
- **"What did you *measurably* improve?"** — Verified: Alorica performance record (awards, adherence, attendance). Client-side metrics are being formalized as case studies with client approval; I don't quote numbers I can't back.

## Questions to Ask Interviewers / Clients

- What does the current workflow look like between first customer contact and resolution or delivery?
- Where does documentation live today, and who actually maintains it?
- What has been tried with automation or AI so far, and what stalled?
- What would a successful first 90 days look like operationally?
