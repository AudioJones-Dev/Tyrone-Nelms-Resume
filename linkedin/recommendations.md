# Recommendations

Strategy for LinkedIn recommendations — who to ask, what they can truthfully attest to, and how to ask. Recommendations are third-party evidence; they must stay consistent with the canonical record.

## Who to Ask (by evidence value)

| Source | Can attest to | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| AJ Digital clients (with their consent) | Consulting approach, workflow improvements, communication | Highest — directly supports current positioning; respect `docs/case-studies.md` gates if the recommendation describes outcomes |
| Florida Ramp & Lift | Field reliability, technical support, operational contribution | High — current, verifiable |
| Miami Spin 360 | Event execution, client-facing delivery, creative production | High — current |

| AHLO | Range, trust across functions, operations direction | Medium |
| Peer consultants / collaborators | Professionalism, systems thinking | Situational |

## What Recommenders Can Say (and not say)

- They may describe anything they directly experienced.
- If a recommender volunteers a metric (e.g., "cut our costs 20%"), that does **not** verify a Tier C claim for our own use — our artifacts still can't cite it until real evidence exists. Their words are theirs.
- Never draft a recommendation containing claims the canonical record contradicts.

## Request Template

> Hi [name] — I'm building out my LinkedIn profile properly and a short recommendation from you would mean a lot. If useful, a direction: you saw my work on [specific project/period] — whatever stood out to you from that is exactly right. Two or three sentences is plenty. And I'd be glad to write one for you too.

Rules for the ask:
- One specific project/period pointer, so the recommendation is concrete.
- Never send a pre-written recommendation for them to sign; suggest a direction at most.
- Always offer reciprocity, and follow through.

## Giving Recommendations

Give them proactively to people whose work was genuinely strong — specific, 3–5 sentences, one concrete observed behavior. Given recommendations appear on the profile and signal generosity and judgment.

## Target State

- 5–8 recommendations spanning the arc: at least one enterprise-era, one current-engagement, one client (gated), one peer.
- Refresh cadence: one new request per quarter until target, then opportunistic.
