# Outreach

Outbound sequences after a connection exists. Two pipelines: consulting (AJ Digital) and job search (Tier 1/2 roles per [`docs/target-roles.md`](../docs/target-roles.md)). Both follow the same rule: every touch adds value or context; none of them pressure.

Square brackets are placeholders. Personalize from a real signal every time.

## Consulting Pipeline (founder-led service businesses)

### Touch 1 — after connection accept (within 2–3 days)

> Thanks for connecting, [name]. No agenda — but since you're running [company], I'm curious: what's the workflow that eats the most time on your team right now? I collect these (occupational habit) and sometimes I can point at a fix in one message.

### Touch 2 — value drop (1–2 weeks later, only if Touch 1 got a reply or clear interest signal)

> That [workflow they mentioned] pattern is common — here's the short version of how I'd approach it: [2–3 sentence practical outline]. If it'd help to go deeper, happy to do a short call; if not, use the outline freely.

### Touch 3 — soft offer (only after real engagement)

> If you want, I can do a quick workflow review of [specific area] — 30 minutes, you leave with a prioritized list either way. Worth it?

**Stop rule:** no reply after two touches → move to nurture (engage with their content occasionally); no further direct messages for at least a quarter.

## Job-Search Pipeline (Tier 1/2 roles)

### Touch 1 — application follow-up (3–5 days after applying)

> Hi [name] — I applied for [role] and wanted to put a face on the application. The short version: 15+ years of service-business operations, Google PM certified, now building AI-enabled workflows for service companies. Glad to share specifics if useful.

### Touch 2 — post-interview thank-you (same day as interview)

> Thank you for the conversation today, [name]. The [specific topic discussed] challenge is exactly the kind of problem I enjoy — [one-line specific thought about it]. Looking forward to next steps either way.

### Touch 3 — graceful close after rejection

> Thanks for letting me know, [name]. I appreciated the process and enjoyed learning about [company/team]. If the picture changes or an operations-shaped problem shows up, I'm easy to find.

## Tracking

Until the Career Intelligence layer exists (planned `career-os/` structure), track outreach minimally per contact: date, pipeline, touch number, response, next action. When that layer lands, this file's tracking rules migrate there.

## Rules

- Truth constraints apply in DMs exactly as in public: no Tier C metrics, no unverified claims, gates on client stories.
- Never send sequenced messages to someone who hasn't engaged; the sequence is a decision tree, not a drip campaign.
- Every message must survive the test: "would I be comfortable if this were screenshotted?"
