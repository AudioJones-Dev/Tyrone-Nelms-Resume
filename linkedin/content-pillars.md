# Content Pillars

The recurring themes all LinkedIn content draws from. Each pillar maps to a positioning pillar in [`docs/career-positioning.md`](../docs/career-positioning.md) and pulls proof only from the canonical records.

## Pillar 1 — Practical AI for Service Businesses

*Positioning pillar: operator-turned-consultant; synthesis stage of the narrative arc.*

- What: where AI actually helps a service business (follow-up, documentation, intake, scheduling) and where it doesn't.
- Example topics: "the workflow to fix before you automate anything"; "what AI can't fix about your follow-up process"; tool-agnostic automation walkthroughs.
- Proof source: AJ Digital work (`experience/aj-digital.md`), qualitative only until case studies clear.

## Pillar 2 — Workflow & Documentation Systems

*Positioning pillar: systems over tools.*

- What: process documentation, SOPs, CRM hygiene, handoff design.
- Example topics: "document the workflow before the tool decides it for you"; ClickUp/Asana/Notion consolidation patterns; why automation without documentation doesn't survive staff changes.
- Proof source: AJ Digital + AHLO operations record.

## Pillar 3 — Operator Lessons

*Positioning pillar: customer-experience literacy; foundation stage of the arc.*

- What: lessons from the support floor, the field, and the warehouse that still apply to modern ops.
- Example topics: what 99% adherence taught about consistency; first-call resolution as a systems principle; what field service teaches about safety and logistics that dashboards miss.
- Proof source: Alorica, UnitedHealthcare, Florida Ramp & Lift records — Tier A/B achievements only.

## Pillar 4 — Building in Public: the Professional OS

*Positioning pillar: evidence discipline.*

- What: how this repository works — canonical records, evidence tiers, gated claims, AI-assisted-but-governed workflows.
- Example topics: "why my professional operating system refuses unverified metrics"; how the strategic layer prevents resume/LinkedIn drift; version-controlling a career.
- Proof source: this repository itself.
- Gate: only if/when the owner decides the repo is publicly promotable (tracked in `featured.md`).

## Pillar 5 — Creative & Brand Operations (minor pillar)

*Positioning pillar: end-to-end service-business range.*

- What: occasional content from the event-production and marketing side; keeps the range visible without diluting the main lane.
- Proof source: Miami Spin 360, AJ Digital marketing work.
- Cadence: no more than ~1 in 6 posts.

## Cadence & Mix

- Sustainable default: 1–2 posts/week; consistency beats volume.
- Rough mix: 30% Pillar 1, 25% Pillar 2, 25% Pillar 3, 15% Pillar 4 (once ungated), 5% Pillar 5.
- Every post uses a structure from [`post-frameworks.md`](post-frameworks.md) and passes its guardrails.
