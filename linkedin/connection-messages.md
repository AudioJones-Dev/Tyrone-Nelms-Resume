# Connection Messages

Templates for LinkedIn connection requests (300-character limit on the note). Philosophy: connect around something real — a post, a role, a shared context. Never pitch in the request.

Square brackets are placeholders; fill them from a real signal before sending. Never send a template unmodified.

## Recruiter / Hiring Manager

> Hi [name] — I saw the [role title] opening at [company]. My background is operations and AI systems for service businesses. Happy to connect either way.

## Founder / Consulting Prospect (warm — engaged with their content)

> Hi [name] — your post about [specific topic] matched what I see with service businesses constantly. I work on exactly that kind of workflow problem. Would enjoy following your work.

## Founder / Consulting Prospect (cold — genuine relevance only)

> Hi [name] — I help founder-led [industry] businesses clean up workflows and put AI to practical use. No pitch — your company came up in [real context] and I'd like to follow what you're building.

## Peer Operator / Consultant

> Hi [name] — fellow operations person here. Your take on [topic] lined up with my experience from [floor/field/campaign context]. Connecting to keep learning from your posts.

## Post-Interview / Post-Meeting

> [Name], thanks for the conversation about [role/project] today. Regardless of where it lands, I enjoyed the discussion about [specific detail]. Glad to stay connected.

## Event / Community Follow-up

> Hi [name] — we crossed paths at [event/community]. Wanted to connect while the context is fresh. Your work on [topic] stood out.

## Rules

- One real, specific detail per message — the placeholder forces it.
- No metrics, no credentials-dumping, no links in the request note.
- If there is no genuine signal to reference, don't send the request yet.
- Follow-up after acceptance belongs to [`outreach.md`](outreach.md), not here.
